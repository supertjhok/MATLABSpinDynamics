# Known Gaps

The Python package is still a porting workspace.

Ported and validated:

- ideal CPMG asymptotic magnetization and echo construction;
- ideal FID acquisition and time-domain trace construction;
- `sim_spin_dynamics_arb10` and the FID-compatible `arb7` path;
- tuned-probe CPMG original/reference asymptotic path;
- untuned-probe CPMG original/reference asymptotic path;
- matched-probe CPMG original/reference asymptotic path;
- ideal-probe finite acquisition with relaxation through
  `calc_macq_ideal_probe_relax4`;
- tuned, untuned, and matched finite-acquisition receiver wrappers;
- public finite ideal CPMG echo-train workflow;
- public CPMG workflow runners returning `CPMGResult`.

Not yet ported:

- newer tuned-probe helper variants outside the original/reference path;
- newer untuned-probe helper variants outside the original/reference path;
- newer matched-probe helper variants outside the original/reference path;
- public finite CPMG train workflows using tuned, untuned, and matched probe
  pulse-shaping and receiver models;
- Q-factor, mistuning, and probe-parameter sweep workflows;
- diffusion kernels;
- imaging workflows;
- OCT and SPA optimization workflows;
- compiled or GPU acceleration backends;
- stable public packaging and autogenerated API reference docs.

The next natural migration target is the relaxation/acquisition layer, followed
by sweep workflows that exercise the existing probe models over Q, frequency
offset, and mistuning parameters. Diffusion and imaging should remain behind
those because they depend on trusted sequence construction and acquisition
semantics.
